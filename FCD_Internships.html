Northeastern Illinois University
Computer Science Department
Addressed to All Internship Sponsors

The Computer Science Internship Program at Northeastern Illinois University has provided successful internships at over a hundred Illinois companies, government agencies, and organizations. Many of our former interns still work for the companies or organizations which provided them with internships.

Internships

  • Can start any time of the year.
  • Can be part-time or full-time.
  • Many are paid but we welcome all internship opportunities
  • The CS department actively solicits internships and acts as an interested and active third party
  • Many students research and line up their own internships

We ask for information to assist you

  • We ask for your requirements and a description of the internship project.
  • Use your own format, send a link on your website, or fill in a form. Request for participation
  • We post your requirements on our website and listserv, and post on a bulletin board
  • The sponsor interviews and selects the best candidate
  • We provide interviewing facilities in cooperation with our Career Placement office.

Students can earn academic credit for internships - normally 3 credit hours, sometimes more

  • This involves the student, the sponsor, and the internship coordinator
  • We sign an Internship Agreement describes the project, learning objectives, and grading criteria
  • Students maintain a portfolio of assignments, work papers, log, and learning summary
  • The interns and internship coordinator meet on a regular basis usually every 2-3 weeks
  • The intern's supervisor critiques the intern.
  • A grade is given at the end of the semester

Details of our Bachelors and Masters Degrees are at:

  • http://homepages.neiu.edu/~compsc/cs_bs_degree_curriculum.html

If you have any questions, please call or send an email.

  • Norm Noerper, Professor and Internship Coordinator, Northeastern Illinois University, Computer Science Department, Chicago, IL 60625
  • n-noerper@neiu.edu
  • 847-975-6794

Thank you for considering Northeastern Illinois University.

Northeastern Illinois University Internships Available

Home STEM Internships

American Chemical Society (ACS)
The ACS provides internship opportunities available in research universities and at companies around the United States.

American Mathematical Society (AMS)
Many research opportunities are available for students majoring in mathematics or with an interest in applied mathematical research. This resource also provides many internship opportunities in industry.

Association of American Medical Colleges
Many of these opportunities are geared towards pre-medical students and students interested in pursuing medical careers, and Ph.D. or M.D./Ph.D. scientists.

American Institute of Physics (AIP)
This resource provides a list of companies that usually have internship opportunities for physics students. In order to apply for the internships, go to each company’s website and apply in their “career opportunities” area.

Google Engineering and Technical Internships
According to Google, "Our interns are a part of Google—involved and solving problems from the start. As a technical intern, you are excited about tackling the hard problems in technology. With internships across the globe, ranging from Software Engineering to User Experience, we offer many opportunities to grow with us."

NSF Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REUs)
The National Science Foundation’s Research Experiences for Undergraduates program opportunities are available for all majors. Many of these experiences are available in partnership with research labs at various academic institutions.

Pathways to Science
About 900 research opportunities are available and listed on the website each year. The current opportunities are useful for all majors.

Summer Research Opportunities Program (SROP)
The goal of the SROP program is to increase the number of underrepresented students who pursue graduate and research careers. Undergraduates from all majors are prepared for graduate schools through research opportunities available at participating universities.


Graduate School Resources

Graduate Resources for Women in STEM is geared toward helping women access this exciting career sector. Because the gender gap is an issue that people are becoming more aware of, a guide has been created to list resources available for women who are underrepresented in this field. This guide provides numerous STEM scholarships for women going into graduate school, tips for helping applications stand out, and support resources for women in the STEM field. There is also a great expert Q&A that addresses common questions for women interested in STEM.

Discover Data Science provides information on graduate school, and certificate and boot camp programs in the field of data science as well as descriptions of potential careers in this area.
